# Phosphor Loop — video-model prompts

Prompts to get a *look* at the process (text-to-video: Sora, Veo, Kling, Runway, Hailuo, Pika).

**How to use these.** Video models render *one short continuous moment* (~4–8s) with *one clear motion*. They do not understand abstract math or long processes. So this is a **shot list** — run each shot separately, then stitch them in order. Prepend the STYLE BLOCK to every shot so they look like one film. Iterate on the shots that matter; regenerate with small wording tweaks until the motion reads right. If a model keeps inventing real-world objects, add: *"abstract scientific animation, no real-world objects, no people, no text."*

---

## STYLE BLOCK  (prepend to every shot)

> Macro scientific-visualization aesthetic. Pitch-black void background. Everything glows in phosphor green (oscilloscope / CRT long-exposure look); moving light leaves persistent trails that slowly fade. Surfaces are dark, wet, viscous — like black ferrofluid and liquid glass. Slow, deliberate, hypnotic motion. Shallow depth of field, soft bloom, fine grain, faint scanline flicker. No people, no text, no UI.

---

## SHOT 1 — The anchor and the restless end
A single fixed point of green light glows at the center of a black void. A thin luminous tether reaches from it to a smaller point that jitters and swings restlessly across the inside of an invisible domed bowl — never stopping, never resting. The far point traces glowing arcs that linger and fade like phosphor. The tether never changes length; only its direction wanders. Camera slowly pushes in.

## SHOT 2 — The tally forms (persistence)
The restless point keeps sweeping across the curved bowl, its glowing trails overlapping and accumulating. Where it lingers, the green brightens and thickens into a luminous bloom; where it rarely goes, the glow stays faint. Over the shot a bright density builds up on the curved surface — a heat-map of light painting where the motion spends its time. Long-exposure light-trail look, no cuts.

## SHOT 3 — Encoding on the little end
All the glowing lines of the swarming motion converge and pass through the single anchor point as if through a pinhole, opening out onto a tiny curved screen just behind it. A shrunken, inverted copy of the bright density blooms into focus on the little screen — the same pattern in miniature. Slow rack focus from the large swarm to the small glowing screen. Soft green bloom.

## SHOT 4 — The landscape, the low point, the drain
Camera pulls back and tilts to reveal the glowing bowl sitting in a vast dark sloped terrain of black viscous liquid. At the bottom of the slope, a still pool of green light — the low point. Off to one side, a slow whirlpool where luminous fluid spirals down into a drain, faint green sparks drawn off and carried away. Slow drift over wet, reflective surfaces.

## SHOT 5 — The pinch (a new anchor buds)
Close on the still green low-point pool. An unseen pull from the side stretches the glowing pool sideways toward the drain; it elongates, thins in the middle, necks like a stretching droplet of liquid light, and pinches off — separating into two glowing pools, the new one offset to the side. Mercury-like surface tension, slow motion, green glow with bloom.

## SHOT 6 — The walk (laying the trench)
A compact glowing dome-and-swarm structure steps slowly across the dark viscous plain like a luminous wind-up toy. With each step it buds a new glowing anchor just ahead of itself and vacates the old one, leaving behind a continuous glowing trench carved into the black surface. The trench is brightest at the front where it is freshly cut and dims toward the back as it cools and sets. Side-tracking camera follows the walk. Time-lapse feel.

## SHOT 7 — The fork (replication)
Overhead view of the glowing trench walking across the black plain. At one point the luminous trail swells, splits, and a second glowing walker buds off sideways and sets out on its own diverging path, cutting a fresh trench of its own. Two luminous trails now advance independently across the dark field. Slow, biological, inevitable. Phosphor green, long-exposure glow, fine grain.

---

## Notes
- **Order matters** for the story: 1→2→3 is encode/read; 4→5 is where a new anchor comes from; 6→7 is walk→replicate.
- If you want ONE hero clip: Shot 5 (the pinch) or Shot 6 (the walk) carries the whole idea visually.
- **Consistency trick**: keep the phrase "phosphor green, black viscous surface, long-exposure glow" identical across shots; reuse the same seed if the model exposes one.
- **Color alt**: swap "phosphor green" for "amber CRT" or "cold cyan" if you want a different signature — just keep it consistent.
- Veo / Sora handle the abstract fluid stuff best as of now; Kling and Hailuo are strong on slow viscous motion; Runway for the light-trail aesthetic. Try the pinch (Shot 5) on two of them and see which reads clearest.
