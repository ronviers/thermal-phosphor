# thermal-phosphor — STATE

*A synthetic reading. The geometry, mechanics, and theory below are established and cited to their fields; assembling them into a blueprint for where a replicator **comes from** is ours, and speculative. We claim the assembly, never the parts, and claim no discovery. The coined words — **the cell, the copy gate, the latch, the pull, the register, closure** — are the mark. Read [`CLAUDE.md`](CLAUDE.md) first (the guards).*

## What this is

A construction-geometry blueprint for **building** — with a compass and a gradient — an object that copies itself well enough to hand off to Darwin. Life read as a **circuit**: every step a gate you can see, no black boxes, no coordinates, no equations — the math is enacted by the stuff, not written down. The arc runs from two tools up to the first **closed, bootstrapping replicator**, and stops there: when selection on heritable variation takes over, TP is done. It builds the object; it does not run Darwin.

One black box remains in the circuit — **fidelity** — and eliminating it is the whole task (§The frontier).

## The kit — two tools, and the medium

- **A gradient — the drive.** Anisotropic: a favored side, broken detailed balance. Not fuel you switch on and off but the object's *constitutive condition* — the organization is held by the flux; cut it and the object relaxes to a fossil. A crystal is **ROM** (holds its state cold, but can never rewrite its own copier); this object is **powered logic** (state carried by the flux, forgets when unpowered, but can carry an *active* copier). Isotropic noise does no directed work — the second law, Feynman–Smoluchowski; the **bias** is what lets the object be an engine. Every gate below runs on this rail.
- **A compass — one constraint.** A stick pinning a reach. Enough to build everything (Mohr–Mascheroni); no straightedge, because flat is just big-and-round. Everything is built from **sphere caps** — the primitive is curved, flatness is the limit.

## The circuit — the gates

The replicator is one loop — **touch · latch · pull · two · touch** — of these gates:

1. **The cell (the bit).** A bistable primitive: a stick that clicks into one of ≥2 discrete configurations — a two-well potential carrying a value a neighbour can read *rightly or wrongly*. Discrete at contact; a continuous smear is below the floor (nothing to be faithful to). *(Home: inversive / Lie-sphere geometry, the medial axis — the massless bit.)*
2. **The copy gate.** A set cell against a blank one biases the blank toward the matching state — templated complementarity, base-pairing with the chemistry filed off. It may be a *bad* copy (fidelity *q* < 1); it may not be *stateless* (it must resolve to a discrete value, not an average). *(Home: von Neumann's constructor–tape unity; templated growth.)*
3. **The latch.** The freshly written cell holds its state long enough to be read onward — a metastable basin, a kinetic trap it can't climb out of unaided. One realization: **jamming** — below the yield threshold a packing sets and keeps. *(This is the persistence primitive, the "phosphor" of the name. Home: granular jamming / kinetic trapping.)*
4. **The pull.** Parent and copy separate into two, discretely. The medium necks and pinches off a second cell offset from the first; geometry forbids sharing a spot, so the copies are forced apart. *(Home: droplet pinch-off, Plateau–Rayleigh.)*
5. **The closure wiring.** Each copy is itself a cell of the same kind, so it templates the next with **no adapter** — output is input, encode and read are the same surface. Not a part: a constraint on how 1–4 are wired. A row of cells is the **register** — the tape. *(If turning a copy back into a template needs separate machinery, a second machine hides in the adapter and the loop is not closed.)*
6. **The fidelity gate — [FRONTIER].** A *driven* cell whose sustained activity **is** its own copier: what it stores and how well it copies are one thing, so a better-copying variant copies *itself* better. This is **strong closure** — fidelity riding on the tape, not set by the bath. It is the only gate not yet drawn, and the only one that can exist *only* in a driven object: a cold cell must offload its fidelity to the bath, which is why crystal copying can be sorted but never bootstrapped.

**The timing rule:** copy fires before the pull separates (copy-then-separate).

## The fidelity sciences, laid alongside the circuit

*Established (Shannon; von Neumann 1956; Hopfield; Ninio; Eigen; the multi-stage proofreading literature) is cited; the reading — that these enter the circuit only as a bound, and that gate 6 is where they must co-realize with the constructor–tape — is ours, and minimal.*

The circuit imports the constructive sciences as a six-gate ladder, but imports the sciences of *reliable copying* as a single number: Eigen's error threshold `N·(1−q) < 1`. That is the only quantitative constraint in the project — gates 1–5 impose none, being qualitative (discrete, templated, metastable, pinched, closed). Yet reliable copying is a developed body — Shannon's noisy-channel coding theorem (1948), von Neumann's *reliable computation from unreliable components* (1956), and Hopfield–Ninio kinetic proofreading (1974; 1975) — and it holds not a bound but a **constructive ladder**, never laid alongside the circuit:

- **Equilibrium discrimination** sets an error floor: a wrong monomer is less stable, giving a ratio `d` of wrong-to-right off-rates. The passive floor, unbeatable at detailed balance.
- **Kinetic proofreading** pushes error *below* the equilibrium floor, and only by spending drive on an irreversible discard branch — one dissipative stage (Hopfield 1974; Ninio 1975).
- **Stacked independent stages** multiply discrimination toward `dⁿ`; a mere delay is not a stage — it lets the binding re-equilibrate rather than resetting (multi-stage proofreading — Murugan–Huse–Leibler; Sartori–Pigolotti).
- **Unbounded fidelity** therefore needs unboundedly many independent stages acting on one site — hence `n` elements *in contact* with it — which a bounded local neighbourhood cannot supply in one dimension. *This last step forces **folding**, and it is the one rung that is ours: the C5 finding (§The frontier).*

Read as a descent — capacity, `n` stages, one stage, the `d`-floor — this is the counterpart ladder that meets the constructive circuit at gate 6: the missing half of the picture, and it was missing because reliable copying entered only as Eigen's number. It also says what gate 6 *is*. Not a seventh mechanism: the point where the proofreading ladder must be realized **in the same string that is the constructor** — von Neumann's self-reproducing automaton, whose tape now both builds the copier and specifies its own proofreading. "Fidelity on the tape" is exactly Hopfield–Ninio proofreading whose discrimination is sequence-set, carried in von Neumann's constructor–tape, held above Eigen's threshold. Three established requirements co-realized in one minimal object.

**The falsifier.** Whether that co-realization is possible with minimal parts — the on-tape proofreading ladder and the closed constructor–tape loop as *one* object. If it is not, no constructed object crosses the error threshold, and TP's claim that the circuit closes up to the Darwinian threshold fails. All three requirements are established and stated forward; nothing is read backward from extant DNA (which, post-Darwin, is Guard-0-suspect). This is the sharp form of "does the constructed object reach the threshold" — replacing the earlier, forensic-contaminated "does bottom-up meet the DNA-strip."

## Why gate 6 is the game — the bootstrap

With fidelity on the tape, a better copier copies itself better, buying a longer register that can encode a still-better copier: **the floor and the ceiling raise each other.** It runs only while it stays above the smear budget — **N·(1−q) < 1** (Eigen, in cartoon form) — a **noise-margin spec on the built loop**: a sloppy gate buys a short register, a tight gate buys a long one. Below the bar the record washes out; above it, it climbs. Cold copies **sort** a standing population; only driven copies **bootstrap.** That difference is the difference between a memory and a replicator.

## The frontier — the one move

Draw gate 6. **What is the minimal driven cell whose activity is its own copier — the least chemistry that puts fidelity on the tape, above the Eigen bar?** Everything else is buildable with a compass on a gradient; this is the transistor to build.

Two leads, both *named, not built* — held in the frontier until one is forced:

- **Sequence-encoded affinity** — the standing lead. A "patchy" cell whose binding strength depends on its own state, so a better-templating variant templates itself better. Open: how little is forced before it amounts to reinventing full base-pairing. *(From the closure-gate report's rescue path; home: templated complementarity.)*
- **Driven multistability** — a driven-native alternative. The scaffold, switched on, settles into one of several maintained circulations; *which* one it holds is the stored value — a bit carried by the flux, not by a static config, so it is constitutively driven (it can't be built as a fossil and switched to a unique answer). *(Home: multistability / attractor selection in dissipative systems.)* **The same wall:** if the attractor is picked by startup fluctuations or by how the drive was applied, its fidelity is set *off the tape* — sortable, not bootstrappable, the identical strong-closure failure the report found for stacking. It relocates gate 6 to a driven bit; it does not clear it. Strong closure still demands that a parent's attractor-choice template *itself* onto the daughter — fidelity riding on which attractor the parent holds.

**The basin reading (a marked cross-check, not a new claim).** The two leads meet the gate-1 home from the dynamics side. An attractor is defined by its *basin*, not its bottom (established dynamical systems); the **watershed** dividing two basins — the locus genuinely undecided between two drains — is the **medial axis** (ridge-between-drains = equidistant-from-boundaries; Blum's medial axis = ridges of the distance transform, Beucher's watershed). So gate 1's massless-bit home (inversive / Lie-sphere, the medial axis) and the driven-multistability lead land on the *same* object — convergence expected, not evidence. It buys a clean restatement of the noise margin: **fidelity = landing the copy on the correct side of a sparse watershed; the Eigen bar = how many ridge-crossings chain before a wrong side gets likely** (re-expressing an import, not a new claim). And it sharpens what gate 1 costs: a smoothing bath alone relaxes to **one** drain (the "render") and stores nothing; a *bit* needs **two** drains with a ridge between — a **fold** (a bifurcation, catastrophe-theory sense) the linear bath never carves. **The brake:** watershed = medial axis is exact only at the **massless, symmetric** limit; a driven, asymmetric well puts its separatrix at the *dynamics* ridge, which is not the geometric medial axis. TP is constitutively massive — so the medial axis is the massless *skeleton* of the cell, and the gap between it and the driven watershed is where the drive-carried bit actually lives. *(The reading is ours — mark; dynamical systems, catastrophe theory, medial-axis/watershed are cited. Note: this "fold" — one basin → two — is **not** the folding of Track A — a chain bending so sequence-distant monomers touch — nor the templating "crease"; three distinct objects, one word, do not unify on the pun.)*

**The fossil-activation test** — a second handle on why gate 6 is special. For each gate ask: can it be assembled cold and switched on, or must the driven state take part in its own formation? Gates 1–5 pass cold (build the scaffold, apply the gradient, it lights up — maintenance). Gate 6 is the one whose organization is *inseparable* from the ongoing driven process — the same claim as "the only gate that can exist only in a driven object," reached from the build side. *(Guard #1's cut-the-drive diagnostic run the other way; convergence is expected, not evidence.)*

**How we attack it — a self-certifying witness challenge ([`CHALLENGE.md`](CHALLENGE.md)).** Gate 6 is closed by a *witness*, not an argument: an explicit minimal driven copier that passes a mechanical **acceptance oracle** whose conditions are the guards as accept/reject predicates — *on the tape* (strong closure), *fidelity vanishes when the drive is cut* (the kill-switch), *heritable and above the Eigen bar*. Formalized, gate 6 = **on-tape kinetic proofreading** (Hopfield–Ninio): drive-elevated *and* sequence-encoded fidelity — the both-yes corner of the grid, the only one that bootstraps rather than sorts. The two leads above are candidate witnesses; the challenge is the standing spec we hand a solver (a model, when one is capable) and verify mechanically — never by refereeing biology. Between now and then the work is hardening the oracle so no off-tape cheat passes. *(Method and target are ours — mark; von Neumann, Eigen, Hopfield–Ninio cited.)*

**The oracle's edges** (self-reflection lives here, not in the challenge — the challenge is dragged to solvers cold). Stress-testing the oracle against a deliberate cheat — a "copier" substring read by a postulated programmable catalyst (the polymerase-postulate) — showed it slipping past C1–C4 while importing the answer. The fix, now folded in, is **C0: no privileged machinery** — the generator must be a fixed, local, translation-invariant, sequence-independent rule, so a "copier" is only ever a substring that raises fidelity under the *one* uniform rule (as a ribozyme does), never a machine that reads the tape. C0 rejects the cheat mechanically and still permits the honest witness. Remaining soft edges: the boundary of "admissible construction" is not yet as sharp as "polynomial maps"; `Q`'s aggregation is fixed to the per-site minimum by fiat. On C0's strength: attempt #1 (log in [`docs/challenge-attempts.md`](docs/challenge-attempts.md)) had a strong solver declare C0 *impossible* to satisfy alongside C2/C4 — but the argument was a **misparse** (it read "rates may depend on local monomer states," the intended mechanism, as the forbidden "rates read a designated substring"). C0 is satisfiable — uniform local physics gives sequence-context-dependent fidelity, and a ribozyme/polymerase is an existence proof — so the fix was to **sharpen** C0's wording, not loosen it. Still open: whether a solver, given the sharpened C0, keeps declaring impossibility (a real signal C0 is too strong) or finally attempts the explicit `W`. Attempt #2 (internal, clamp-motif) then **built a C0-legal witness that passes C0–C4** — settling the impossibility question constructively — but it is *weak*: fidelity is a bounded knob capped at `q_max` by the off-tape discrimination constant `f`, the same stacking-shaped hole (a fidelity ceiling off the tape) in subtler disguise, and it slips through C2 (which tests only *dependence* on the tape, not whether the tape can raise the ceiling). An external witness (attempt #3, Gemini 3.1) then **converged** — independently a clamp-motif Hopfield copier, passes C0–C4, same off-tape ceiling (bounded by `d²`). Two independent witnesses hitting the identical wall promoted **C5 — on-tape capacity** (`sup_T Q = 1`, no off-tape ceiling) from candidate to condition; **C5 is now folded into [`CHALLENGE.md`](CHALLENGE.md)** (both witnesses now fail the full C0–C5 oracle — correctly; the bar sits at the real gate). A latent second gap remains: C3's *letter* (tape-control vanishes at A=0) permits a mostly-*cold* copier — attempt #3's `Q(A=0)=0.990` is off-tape ROM with a thin driven top-up, where attempt #2's `Q(A=0)=½` is fully drive-carried; whether to strengthen C3 to demand the fidelity itself be drive-carried is an open thesis-level call. Harden by attacking the oracle, not by adding clauses speculatively.

**The cascade attempt (attempt #4) cornered the real gate.** A tape-set proofreading cascade (a run of `m` clamps → `m` driven stages → `dᵐ`) reaches `sup_T Q → 1`, so **C5 is satisfiable** — but it exposes two things the oracle now hangs on. *(A) C0-vs-C5 tension:* unbounded per-site fidelity under a bounded-window local rule forces a **processive** tape-length proofreading track — which is exactly the polymerase C0's forbidden-shortcut targets. C0's letter permits it, its spirit is strained; **this tension is gate 6's real difficulty** (minimal magic vs reinventing base-pairing), now surfaced by the oracle rather than argued. *(B) Degenerate pass:* an all-`1` tape self-proofreads to `Q→1` and passes C0–C5 while carrying zero information — so the oracle does not yet demand **von Neumann self-description** (candidate **C6**: the copier must be a description that constructs the copier, not a pattern that copies itself). The live fork: does gate 6 *permit* a minimal processive copier (task → write the smallest one), or does strict C0 forbid processivity — making "some processive/programmable machinery is *forced*" the honest answer to *how little chemistry*?

**Ruling (2026-07-22): processivity is in-bounds iff it *emerges* from the local rule** (a latching chain-reaction — a site locks only once its neighbor has; complementary monomers latch), out-of-bounds if it is a postulated read-head that slides the tape. Folded into C0. This **disqualifies attempt #4's cascade** (a sliding token). And it makes C5 look *unreachable* under the present grammar: emergent single-pass latching gives O(1) proofreading per site → bounded fidelity → the clamp-motif ceiling again. **The one escape is folding** — complementary latching brings *sequence-distant* monomers into *spatial* contact, so a bounded **3-D** window can hold a deep, sequence-encoded catalytic pocket (a ribozyme's active site) even though its elements are far apart along the tape. So C5 is reachable, but only if "bounded window" means bounded in space-after-folding, not along the 1-D sequence. **Finding:** the challenge's grammar `(S,N,c,A,W)` is 1-D and abstracted the geometry away — which is *why* C5 looked impossible. Gate 6's real object is a **minimal self-folding sequence-encoded catalyst**, and it needs the geometry back — i.e. thermal-phosphor's original kit (sphere caps, the medial axis, folding). The oracle has driven the abstract challenge back to the geometric substrate TP started from. **Triangulated (attempt #5):** an independent external cascade (Gemini) is emergent/in-bounds but also fails C5 — its downstream-gated wait is a *delay line* (saturates at ~`d²`, independent of `n`), not a stack of independent stages. So both cascade attempts fail C5 from opposite sides (mine: right mechanism, forbidden sliding token; Gemini's: in-bounds, wrong mechanism), and both point to folding as the only way to put `n` independent stages in contact with one site. Direction confirmed: **extend to a folding grammar (Option A = the math proof), keep TP's geometric substrate as the evolutionary engine (Option B).** Track A's v0 is [`CHALLENGE-fold.md`](CHALLENGE-fold.md); the 1-D [`CHALLENGE.md`](CHALLENGE.md) is closed (it proved its point). The fork and next steps are in [`docs/handoff_next_session.md`](docs/handoff_next_session.md).

## Working backwards — the lab has both ends

Gate 6 is not exotic; it is a **gap between two real, working systems**. Scoring existing driven matter against the circuit locates it:

- **The drive half, in a beaker:** *chemically-fueled dissipative self-assembly* (Boekhoven & van Esch; Prins) — structures that persist only while fuel flows and relax when it is cut. This is powered-logic / fossil-when-cut exactly (the constitutive gradient), and it *passes the fossil-activation test in the telling direction*: you cannot build it cold and switch it to a unique state. But it **does not copy** — it supplies the *rail* (the constitutive drive, the latch-while-fueled) and nothing that reads or writes: no discrete cell, no register, no heredity.
- **The copy half, in a beaker:** *cross-catalytic RNA replicators* (Lincoln & Joyce 2009) — two ribozymes each catalyzing the other's assembly, self-sustaining, exponentially amplifying, and **evolving in vitro**. Fidelity is sequence-set, so it clears **strong closure** and reaches the Darwinian threshold — but with *full molecular chemistry*, the endpoint TP wants the **least** of.

Gate 6 is the **minimal bridge** between them: take the dissipative-assembly system (drive-half, already fossil-activation-positive) and add the smallest thing that makes *which assembly it settles into* template itself onto a daughter — the driven-multistability lead made concrete. That is the one move, restated as a lab question. *(A reading of existing systems, not a built toy — guard #4.)*

## Imports (cite, never rebuild; the reading is ours)

- **The loop** — von Neumann: constructor and tape as one object.
- **The noise margin** — Eigen's error threshold / quasispecies.
- **Reliable copying** — fidelity as its own developed body, so far imported only as the noise margin above: kinetic proofreading — fidelity below the equilibrium floor costs dissipation (Hopfield 1974; Ninio 1975); multi-stage proofreading cascades — independent stages multiply discrimination, a delay does not (Murugan–Huse–Leibler; Sartori–Pigolotti); reliable computation from unreliable components (von Neumann 1956); channel capacity and error-correcting codes (Shannon 1948). von Neumann owns both this and the loop.

## Scope — where it stops

TP hands off at the **Darwinian threshold** (Woese) — the moment there is a copied, heritable, selectable record above the bar, where selection on heritable variation takes over. The object built up to that line is a **unit of evolution** — *multiplication + heredity + heritable variation* (Maynard Smith–Szathmáry; equivalently the *minimal Darwinian individual*, Godfrey-Smith) — and those three are exactly gates **4** (the pull → multiplication), **5** (closure → heredity), and **6** (fidelity on the tape → *heritable* variation). TP claims the circuit *can* close up to that entity; it does not model what selection does after. And (Guard 0) it does not ask *when*, *how often*, or *why-once* any of this occurred — those are questions about a history, not about buildability.
